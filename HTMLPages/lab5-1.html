Works of Vincent Van Gogh

The Starry Night

Vincent van Gogh painted Starry Night in 1889 during his stay at the assylm of Saint-Paul-de-Mausole near Saint-Remy-de-Provence. Van Gogh lived in the hospital; he was allowed more freedoms that any of the other patients. If attended, he could leave the hospital grounds; he was allowed to paint, read, and widthdraw into his own room.

Sunflowers

Van Gogh's paintings of Sunflowers are among his most famous. He did them in Arles, in the south of France, in 1888 and 1889. Vincent painted a total of five large canvases with sunflowers in a vase, with three of yellow 'and nothing else'. In this way, he demonstrated that it was possible to create an image with numerous variants of a single color, wihtout an loss of eloquence.
